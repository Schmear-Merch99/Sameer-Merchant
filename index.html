Merchant Tutoring

My Story

In 10th grade, I started tutoring students in math for free through my high school’s math club and at Kumon as a grader. One day, a student came to my tutoring session and I was going over graphing sine and cosine with him and how to do that. I quickly realized he did not know anything about the unit circle, which is important for graphing these functions without a calculator. I went over the unit circle with him and he quickly understood how to graph the two functions. This made tutoring much easier as we went over subsequent topics.

As I went through college as an engineering student, I realized that the problems that students had with math earlier made their time at college much more difficult. Students who had not taken any sort of calculus in high school found themselves struggling in “weed out” introduction math classes. Because of this, I became a tutor and teaching assistant at my university amidst the pandemic.

Since college is online for most students, I tutored students using a writing tablet that was provided to me by my university. I felt a sense of enjoyment in my tutoring sessions and office hours, which lead me to create my own tutoring business.

Why choose me?

One thing that would differentiate me from other tutors is I talk about the applications of some of the topics, not just the material itself. It is my belief that learning about why the material being taught is so important creates more passion in the student to learn more. In the past, I have worked as a tutor and a teaching assistant for many classes including Computer Architecture, Systems Software, and Linear methods and from those experiences, I have learned about the best strategies for teaching students the material.

What do I tutor?

Subjects I tutor include but are not limited to SAT Math, AP Physics C: Mechanics and Electricity and Magnetism, Introduction to circuits, K-12 Mathematics, AP Calculus AB and BC (Calculus 1 and 2), Linear Algebra, and Differential Equations.
